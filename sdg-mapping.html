Waste management for industries

SDG Goal 12: Responsible consumption and production-Ensuring sustainable consumption and production patterns.

SDG Target 12.4: Achieve the environmentally sound management of chemicals and all wastes throughout their life cycle to minimize adverse impacts on human health and the environment, in accordance with agreed international frameworks.

SDG Indicator 12.4.2: Hazardous waste generated per capita and proportion of hazardous waste treated, by type of treatment.

Industrial waste can contaminate water bodies, air, and soil. Effective waste management protects Chitranagari's natural landscapes and agricultural lands that support the local economy.

Industrial pollution poses risk to historic monuments and artifacts through corrosion and environmental degradation. Managing waste ensures the longevity of Chitranagari's cultural legacy.

The initiative supports industries in adopting cleaner technologies and practices, fostering economic growth without compromising the environment.

Import and export of goods

SDG Goal 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure

Relevant SDG Target: Target 9.1: Develop quality, reliable, sustainable, and resilient infrastructure, including regional and transborder infrastructure, to support economic development and human well-being, with a focus on affordable and equitable access for all.

Relevant SDG Indicator: Indicator 9.1.2: Passenger and freight volumes, by mode of transport.

This goal emphasizes building resilient infrastructure, promoting inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and fostering innovation, which directly aligns with improving logistics and trade systems for Chitranagari.
